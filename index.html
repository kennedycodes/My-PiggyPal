Your Path to Financial Freedom

At PiggyPal, we believe that financial management shouldn't be stressful or complicated. Our mission is to empower you to take control of your finances and achieve a balanced, fulfilling life. We employ the proven 50/30/20 budgeting strategy to help you manage your money effectively, ensuring you can cover your essentials, enjoy your desires, and secure your future.

How It Works:

  1. Income Calculation
    • Post-Tax Income: If you know your post-tax annual income, simply enter it, and we'll get started.
    • Pre-Tax Income: If you only have your pre-tax income, no problem. We'll calculate your post-tax income for you, ensuring accuracy based on your specific tax situation.
  2. Budget Creation
    • 50% Needs: Essentials like housing, utilities, groceries, transportation, and insurance. These are the non-negotiables that keep your life running smoothly.
    • 30% Wants: Lifestyle choices such as dining out, entertainment, hobbies, and travel. This category ensures you enjoy life without guilt.
    • 20% Savings and Debt Repayment: Building your financial future through savings, investments, and paying down debt. This is your path to long-term security and peace of mind.
  3. Personalized Subcategories
    • Under each main category, we offer customizable subcategories. Tailor your budget to reflect your unique spending habits and priorities.
  4. Track Your Spending
    • Our intuitive tracking tools allow you to monitor your acual spending against your budget. Visual dashboards and reports help you stay on track, identify patterns, and make adjustments as needed.

Debt Prioritization

Debt Snowball Method

  • List Debts by Balance: List all debts from smallest to largest balance, regardless of interest rate.
  • Make Minimum Payments: Continue making minimum payments on all debts except the smallest one.
  • Focus on the Smallest Debt: Allocate any extra money to pay off the smallest debt as quickly as possible.
  • Move to the Next Debt: Once the smallest debt is paid off, move to the next smallest debt, adding the payment you were making on the first debt to the payments on the second one.
  • Repeat the Process: Continue this process until all debts are paid off.

Debt Avalanche Method

  • List Debts by Interest Rate: List all debts from highest to lowest interest rate, regardless of balance.
  • Make Minimum Payments: Continue making minimum payments on all debts except the one with the highest interest rate.
  • Focus on the Highest Interest Debt: Allocate any extra money to pay off the debt with the highest interest rate as quickly as possible.
  • Move to the Next Highest Interest Debt: Once the highest interest debt is paid off, move to the next highest interest rate debt, adding the payment you were making on the first debt to the payments on the second one.
  • Repeat the Process: Continue this process until all debts are paid off.

Tips & Tricks For Tackling Debt

  • Pay Minimum Payments: Ensure you continue making at least the minimum payments on all your debts to avoid late fees and penalties, even while focusing on one debt at a time.
  • Automate Payments: Set up automatic payments to ensure you never miss a due date, avoiding late fees and protecting your credit score.
  • Make Extra Payments: Whenever possible, make extra payments towards your debt to reduce the principal faster and save on interest.
  • Cut Unnecessary Expenses: Identify and reduce non-essential spending to free up more money for debt repayment.
  • Avoid New Debt: Refrain from taking on new debt while you're in the process of paying off existing balances.
